    • Hado Shoryuken
    • Forbidden Shoryuken
  • A jumping kick in which I rotate my legs in a hurricane-like formation
    • An upgraded, stationary Tatsumaki Senpukyaku with an added vacuum effect, allowing me to suck in nearby opponents and hit them multiple times
  • An energy projectile created from my willpower. Those hit by it feel the force of a staggering kick with lingering pain.
    • A Hadoken imbued with the power of fire. Acts as a separate move in SFII and Alpha, and as my EX Hadoken from the III series onward
    • A chargeable, electrified version of the Shinku Hadoken. Those hit with a fully-charged blast are stunned, giving me another opening to attack
    • A powerful, multi-hitting Hadoken which can beat out smaller or weaker projectiles
    • A variant of the Shinku Hadouken done with the Satsui no Hado. I can perform this without transforming, but still retains this as Evil Ryu
    • Ren Hadoken
    • Baku Hadoken
    • Hado no Kamae
    • Hashogeki
  • A slow yet hard-hitting forward-stepping side kick. More powerful versions of this move can send opponents flying off walls
  • My V-Skill, based on the parry mechanic from Street Fighter III and the Hanagashi move given to me in USFIV’s Omega Mode. I enter a stance, during which he can parry an incoming attack
  • My first V-Trigger. All of Ryu’s moves, in particular the Hadoken and Shoryuken, gain electric properties. I can charge up my Hadoken to break through an opponent’s defenses, and my Shinkuu Hadoken is automatically upgraded to the Denjin Hadoken in this state, although performing it ends the V-Trigger
  • My second V-Trigger. He gains access to a counterattack called Isshin, which forces opponents into a crumple state and leaves them open to follow-up attacks
  • An overhead punch primarily meant to break the guard of crouching opponents
  • A crushing blow to the enemy’s midsection
